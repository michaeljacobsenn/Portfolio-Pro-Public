Setup

Set the app up around your real week.

The app is strongest when it understands your cash timing. That means paycheck day, recurring bills, debt due dates, and the minimum cash cushion you do not want to cross.

01

Bring in your numbers

Link accounts with Plaid or enter everything manually. You only need enough detail to reflect checking, savings, debt, bills, and the accounts you actually use.

02

Set your floor and pay rhythm

Add your payday, paycheck amount, weekly allowance, and emergency floor. This is the logic that helps the audit tell you whether the week is green, yellow, or red.

03

Load debt and renewals

Add APRs, limits, promo dates, annual fees, and recurring charges. That gives the app enough structure to prioritize the right move instead of just showing balances.

Weekly Audit

What the audit actually looks at.

The audit is not one score pulled from thin air. It blends local money math, your timing rules, and AI reasoning to turn a messy financial picture into a short action list.

Safety and timing

  • Checking runway until the next paycheck or known cash inflow.
  • Whether upcoming bills or renewals threaten your floor.
  • How much room is actually safe for debt payoff, savings, or discretionary spend.

Debt and allocation logic

  • Which card or loan is the smartest target based on APR, promo windows, and cash safety.
  • Where cash should go first when multiple priorities are competing.
  • What to wait on so one aggressive move does not create next-week damage.

Context from the rest of the app

  • Budget targets and actual spend behavior.
  • Net worth, cash, debt, and market-position trends over time.
  • Renewal timing, annual fees, and cash flow calendar pressure points.

How to use the result

  • Read the headline first: green, yellow, or red week.
  • Do the first one or two recommended moves before chasing optimization.
  • Use AskAI for follow-ups like "what happens if I pay this card Friday?"

Privacy

Use it without handing over your whole financial life.

Your core financial data lives on your device. When you use shipped AI features, the app sends scrubbed prompts through the Catalyst Cash backend proxy to the selected model provider. Raw financial payloads are not retained on our servers.

Local-first

Core data stays on-device

Accounts, debts, renewals, settings, and history are stored locally by default.

Secure proxy

AI requests are scrubbed first

Sensitive patterns are cleaned before prompts are sent through the backend to the chosen model.

Backups

Manual exports leave secrets out

Passcodes, API keys, device IDs, and subscription state are excluded from manual backups.

FAQ

Common questions before people commit.

Do I need Plaid?

No. Plaid is optional. Manual entry is fully supported and the core weekly audit still works.

Should I rerun the audit every day?

No. Weekly is the default rhythm. Rerun it when balances, bills, paychecks, or plans materially change.

Is this financial advice?

No. It is educational software and AI-generated analysis, not fiduciary or professional financial advice.

What is the best first setup?

Checking, savings, debt balances, recurring bills, payday, and a real floor. That is enough to get useful output quickly.
